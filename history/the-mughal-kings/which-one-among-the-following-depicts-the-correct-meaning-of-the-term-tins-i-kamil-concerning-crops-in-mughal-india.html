Which one among the following depicts the correct meaning of the term Tins-i-Kamil concerning crops in Mughal India?

(A) ** Cash crop

(B) Crop grown, in the arid

(C) Paddy crop

(D) Coarse crop

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The term Jins-i Kamil literally means perfect crops. The Mughal state also encouraged peasants to cultivate such crops as they brought in more revenue. Crops such as cotton and sugarcane were Jins-i Kamil par excellence.

Concept note-2: -The Mughal state also encouraged peasants to cultivate jins-i kamil (literally, perfect crops) as they brought in more revenue. Crops such as cotton and sugarcane were jins-i kamil par excellence.

Concept note-3: -From among the following which one depicts the correct meaning of the term Jins-i-kamil concerning crops in Mughal India? Option B is correct because Jins-i-kamil stands for perfect crops. Cotton, sugarcane is considered as perfect crop.

Concept note-4: -Agriculture. Indian agricultural production increased under the Mughal Empire. A variety of crops were grown, including food crops such as wheat, rice, and barley, and non-food cash crops such as cotton, indigo and opium.

Concept note-5: -Cultivation of rice, jute, tea, sugarcane, and wheat are usually called cash crops.
