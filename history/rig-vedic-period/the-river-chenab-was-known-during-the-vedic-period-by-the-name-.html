HISTORY

RIG VEDIC PERIOD

[SOURCES]
The river Chenab was known during the Vedic period by the name :

(A) Parushni

(B) ** Askini

(C) Vitasta

(D) Shutudri

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Name. The Chenab river was called Asikni (Sanskrit: ) in the Rigveda (VIII. 20.25, X. 75.5).

Concept note-2: -Chenab was known as ‘Askini’ in the Rig Vedic Period. This river is traditionally known as Chandrabhaga river. The name meant that it was seen to have dark-coloured waters.

Concept note-3: -The Ravi river is known as Parushni in the Vedic corpus. It is also referred to as Iravati. It is part of the Indus river system. The river rises in the Bara Bhangal, Himachal Pradesh and drains into the Arabian sea.

Concept note-4: -Vitasta is a Rigvedic name given to River jhelum. Or in Rigveda river jhelum was called Vitasta.

Concept note-5: -Jhelum was Vitasta. Chenab was Chandrabhaga. For the other two, original names still used. Ravi is a short form of Iravati.
