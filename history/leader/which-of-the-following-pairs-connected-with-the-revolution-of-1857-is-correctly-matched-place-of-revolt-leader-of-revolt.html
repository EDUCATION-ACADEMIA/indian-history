Which of the following pairs connected with the Revolution of 1857 is correctly matched? Place of Revolt: Leader of Revolt

(A) ** Lucknow: Begum Hazrat Mahal

(B) Kanpur: Rani Lakshmibai

(C) Jhansi: Nana Saheb

(D) Delhi: Tantiya Tope

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The correct answer is Kanpur: Begum Hazrat Mahal .

Concept note-2: -Detailed Solution The correct answer is C. The leader in Mathura during the 1857 Revolt is Devi Singh. Kadam Singh was the leader of Meerut during the 1857 Revolt. Hence option C is not correctly matched.

Concept note-3: -Hazrat Mahal led the 1857 revolt in Lucknow.

Concept note-4: -The storm-centres of the revolt were Delhi, Kanpur, Lucknow, Bareilly, Jhansi and Arrah.

Concept note-5: -Begum Hazrat Mahal (c. 1820 – 7 April 1879), also known as the Begum of Awadh, was the second wife of Nawab of Awadh Wajid Ali Shah, and the regent of Awadh in 1857–1858. She is known for the leading role she had in the rebellion against the British East India Company during the Indian Rebellion of 1857.
