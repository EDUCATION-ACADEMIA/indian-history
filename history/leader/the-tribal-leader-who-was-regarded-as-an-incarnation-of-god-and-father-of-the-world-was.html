The tribal leader who was regarded as an incarnation of God and father of the world was

(A) Nanak Bhil

(B) Siddhu Santha

(C) ** Birsa Munda

(D) Jagia Bhagat

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Birsa and his disciples set themselves the task of serving the famine-stricken and sick people. He became a legend during his lifetime and was called “Dharati Aba” (father of the earth) and was worshipped as “Birsa Bhagwan”.

Concept note-2: -Birsa Munda: The tribal folk hero who was God to his people by the age of 25.

Concept note-3: -Birsa Munda’s spirit of activism in the late 19th century is considered one of the strongest marks of protest against British rule in India. Known by his tribe and followers as ‘Bhagwan’, the tribal leader is deified and venerated as a ‘God’ by tribal people as far away as Mysore and Kodagu districts in Karnataka.

Concept note-4: -Birsa Munda pronunciation (help·info) (15 November 1875 – 9 June 1900) was an Indian tribal freedom fighter, and folk hero who belonged to the Munda tribe.

Concept note-5: -Alluri Sitaram Raju: He led a movement of the tribal people in the Gudem hills of Andhra Pradesh. Many people considered him as an incarnation of God.
