MISCELLENOUS QUESTIONS

[SOURCES]
The earliest example of a landgrant is provided by an inscription

(A) the guptas

(B) the mahameghavahanas

(C) the mauryas

(D) ** the satavahanas

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Despite the fact that the oldest mention of gifts of land appears in Later Vedic texts, the Satavahana and Kshatrapa epigraphs discovered in Nashik are the earliest stone inscriptions that document land grants with tax exemptions. But it was in the fourth century CE that the practice really began to expand.

Concept note-2: -Land Grants in Gupta period. Grants of land were provided to religious and ritual specialists, as well as to officers and other government officials.

Concept note-3: -The Satavahanas were also the first rulers to make land grants to Brahmanas, although we find more instances of such grants being made to Buddhist monks. The Satavahanas adopted the policy of promoting immunities to Brahmanas and monks, mainly to promote agriculture.

Concept note-4: -(i) The land grants give us a glimpse of relationship between the state and the farmers. (ii) Certain land grants for example gives us details of things and products to be given or not to be given to someone. By this we can deduce what people produced, their subsistence etc.
