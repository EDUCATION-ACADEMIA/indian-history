Who is the author of Prithviraj Raso?

(A) ** Chandabardai

(B) Gunadhya

(C) Samdeva

(D) Amir Khusro

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Authorship and date. According to tradition, the Prithviraj Raso was composed by Chand Bardai, Prithviraj’s court poet (raj kavi), who accompanied the king in all his battles. The last canto, which narrates the death of Chand Bardai and Prithviraj, is said to have been composed by Chand Bardai’s son Jalha (or Jalhan).

Concept note-2: -The Prithviraj Raso (IAST: Pṛthvīrāja Rāso) is a Braj language epic poem about the life of Prithviraj Chauhan (reign. c. 1177–1192 CE). It is attributed to Chand Bardai, who according to the text, was a court poet of the king.

Concept note-3: -Chand Bardai (circa 1200, September 30, 1150(1150-11-30)) was the court poet of king Prithviraj Chauhan, who ruled Ajmer and Delhi from 1165 to 1192. His original name was Prithvi Chand.

Concept note-4: -Detailed Solution Chand Bardai wrote, “Prithviraj Raso” which is a poem about Prithviraj Chauhan.

Concept note-5: -Chand Bardai was an Indian writer, who created Prithviraj Raso, an epic sonnet in Braj Bhasha about the life of the Chauhan lord Prithviraj Chauhan.
