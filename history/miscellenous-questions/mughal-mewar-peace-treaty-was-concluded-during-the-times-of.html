Mughal-Mewar peace treaty was concluded during the times of?

(A) Akbar

(B) Humayun

(C) ** Jahangir

(D) Shah Jahan

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Mughal-Mewar treaty was signed between Jahangir and Amar Singh on 5th February 1615. Reign of Amar Singh is from 1597 to 1620 A.D. The proposal of the treaty was presented by Haridas Jhala and Shubhkaran from the side of Mewar.

Concept note-2: -Treaty in 1615 In the treaty, it was agreed that: The ruler of Mewar, will not be bound to present himself in person at Mughal court, instead, a relative of the Rana would wait upon the Mughal Emperor and serve him. It was also agreed that the Ranas of Mewar would not enter matrimonial relations with the Mughals.

Concept note-3: -In 1681, Rana Jai Singh, the new Rana of Mewar, signed a peace treaty with Aurangzeb. Jai Singh fought a series of battles against Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb. Aurangzeb and the Rana signed a peace treaty, and Aurangzeb turned his attention to the Rathores of Marwar and the Marathas in the Deccan, In 1681.

Concept note-4: -As a result, in 1615, an agreement was finally reached between Rana Amar Singh, son of Maharana Pratap Singh and Jahangir. Under this agreement, Jahangir returned all the land area of Mewar and the fort of Chittor to Rana, but the condition was that he would not strengthen the fort of Chittor.

Concept note-5: -Solution : In the reign of Mughal Emperor Jahangir the treaty of Chittor was signed. This treaty was concluded between Rana Amar Singh and Jahangir by which Rana accepted sovereignty of Mughal Empire over Chittor.
