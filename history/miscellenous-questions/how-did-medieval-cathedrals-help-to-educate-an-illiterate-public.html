How did medieval cathedrals help to educate an illiterate public?

(A) ** They held religious education classes.

(B) They required Church members to learn and to read the Bible.

(C) They supported tax-based public education.

(D) They featured sculptures and stained glass that portrayed biblical stories.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Cathedral Building As an Expression of Faith The building of monumental cathedrals in the middle ages was a reflection of faith and the channel for much of the creative energy of medieval European society. Although cathedral building was driven by religious figures or institutions, it was often a community effort.

Concept note-2: -Education: The church established primary schools as well as more specialized institutions to train future clergy. These specialized institutions developed into Universities. 2. Most medical care was provided in monastries run by the church.

Concept note-3: -Cathedrals and chapels not only provide a space for worship, but they are also vessels for the display of religious iconography and art.

Concept note-4: -They used royal feet. There were master quarrymen, plasterers, mortar-makers, stone cutters and masons. Then there were the men that were common laborers. Most of the men worked on the buildings because the church told them that if they helped build the cathedrals, their sins would be forgiven.
