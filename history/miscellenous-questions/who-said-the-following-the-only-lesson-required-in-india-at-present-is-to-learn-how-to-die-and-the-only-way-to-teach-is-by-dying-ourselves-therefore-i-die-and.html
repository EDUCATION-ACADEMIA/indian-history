Who said the following “the only lesson required in India at present is to learn how to die and the only way to teach is by dying ourselves. Therefore, I die and glory in my martyrdom”?

(A) Khudiram Bose

(B) Chandra Shekhar Azad

(C) ** Madan Lal Dhingra

(D) Aurbindo Ghosh

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The end of the statement written by Dhingra that was published after his execution. Extract: The only lesson required in India today is to learn how to die and the only way to teach it is by dying ourselves, and so, I die and glory in my Martyrdom. Bande Mataram.

Concept note-2: -Statement of Dhingra before Pronouncement of Verdict I do not want to say anything in defence of myself, but simply to prove the justice of my deed. As for myself, no English law court has got any authority to arrest and detain me in prison, or pass sentence of death on me.

Concept note-3: -Madan Lal Dhingra was born on 18 September, 1883 in Amritsar, Punjab. He was a freedom fighter. He had taken his primary education in 1904 he lead a student protest against Principal’s order to have college blazer made out of Imported Cloth from England. He was thrown out of college.

Concept note-4: -In 1909, Madanlal Dhingra shot dead Sir William Wyllie near Trafalgar Square in London. Madanlal Dhingra was hung as a consequence of his actions.
