EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ibn Battuta was a Moroccan traveller who widely travelled the medieval world. Ibn Battuta visited the court of Muhammad Bin Tughlaq who patronised various scholars.

Concept note-2: -Ibn Battuta was a traveller who visited India during the Sultanate period.

Concept note-3: -C) Ibn Battuta came to India at the time of the rule of the Delhi Sultanate. Muhammad Bin Tughlaq was the ruler of India at that time.

Concept note-4: -The Rihla, formal title A Masterpiece to Those Who Contemplate the Wonders of Cities and the Marvels of Travelling, is the travelogue written by Ibn Battuta, documenting his lifetime of travel and exploration, which according to his description covered about 70, 000 miles (110, 000 km).
