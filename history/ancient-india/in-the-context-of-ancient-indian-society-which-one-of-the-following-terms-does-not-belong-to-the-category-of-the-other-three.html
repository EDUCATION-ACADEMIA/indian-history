In the context of ancient Indian society, Which one of the following terms does not belong to the category of the other three?

(A) Kula

(B) Vamsa

(C) ** Kosa

(D) Gotra

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Kosa was utilized for depository and the remaining of the three terms were identified with family. So, the correct answer is Option C.

Concept note-2: -Notes: Kosa was used for treasury and the rest of the three terms were related to family.

Concept note-3: -Ans : There was a caste system with four major groups in early Indian Society. Brahmins or priests, Kshatriyas or warriors or aristocrats, Vaishyas or peasants or merchants and Shudras, or serfs. This division of people by class and occupation within India grew even more.

Concept note-4: -The village, the caste system, and the family are considered the three pillars of Indian society. Historically, India has been comprised of small villages that had autonomy, or the right to self-govern.

Concept note-5: -8. What are the divisions in ancient Indian culture? Answer: The History of India begins with the Indus Valley Civilization and the coming of the Aryans. These two phases are generally described as the pre-Vedic and Vedic periods.
