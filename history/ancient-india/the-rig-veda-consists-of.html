The Rig Veda consists of

(A) 1000 hymns

(B) ** 1028 hymns

(C) 500 hymns

(D) 2000 hymns

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Rig Veda consists of 1028 hymns, organised into ten books known as maṇḍalas. Each maṇḍala has sūktas (hymns) formed by individual strophes called ṛc (ric) from which the name Rig Veda.

Concept note-2: -1900–1200 BCE has also been given. The text is layered, consisting of the Samhita, Brahmanas, Aranyakas and Upanishads. The Rigveda Samhita is the core text and is a collection of 10 books (maṇḍalas) with 1, 028 hymns (sūktas) in about 10, 600 verses (called ṛc, eponymous of the name Rigveda).

Concept note-3: -This Veda is a collection of 1028 hymns grouped into ten books (mandalas). It was preserved orally before it was written down at about 300 BCE. This Veda is one of the oldest sacred books of Hinduism.

Concept note-4: -The Rigveda includes more than a thousand hymns, called sukta. These hymns are in praise of various gods and goddesses.

Concept note-5: -About Rig Veda Rig Veda was said to be composed between 1500-1200 B.C, also known as the ‘first testament of mankind’. It is a collection of 10, 600 verses and 1, 028 hymns. It consists of 10 books also known as mandals, each mandal consists of Suktas.
