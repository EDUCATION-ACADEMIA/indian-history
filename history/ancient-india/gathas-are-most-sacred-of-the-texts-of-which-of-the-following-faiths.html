Gathas are most sacred of the texts of which of the following faiths?

(A) ** Zoroastrianism

(B) Jainism

(C) Confucianism

(D) Buddhism

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Zoroastrianism, the religion of the ancient Iranians, is named after Zarathushtra (Zoroaster) whose hymns (Gathas) are thought to have been composed 1500–1000 BCE.

Concept note-2: -What are the key sacred texts of Zoroastrianism? These religious ideas are encapsulated in the sacred texts of the Zoroastrians and assembled in a body of literature called the Avesta.

Concept note-3: -The Gathas (/ˈɡɑːtəz, -tɑːz/) are 17 Avestan hymns traditionally believed to have been composed by the prophet Zarathushtra (Zoroaster). They form the core of the Zoroastrian liturgy (the Yasna). They are arranged in five different modes or metres.

Concept note-5: -Definition. The Avesta is the scripture of Zoroastrianism which developed from an oral tradition founded by the prophet Zoroaster (Zarathustra, Zartosht) sometime between c. 1500-1000 BCE.
