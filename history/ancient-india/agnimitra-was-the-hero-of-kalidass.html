Agnimitra was the hero of Kalidas’s

(A) Sakuntala

(B) ** Malavikagnimitra

(C) Megadutta

(D) Mrichhakatikam

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hint: The second king of the Shunga dynasty of northern India was Agnimitra. In 149 BC, he succeeded his father, Pushyamitra Shunga . Complete answer: Agnimitra was the hero of the Malavikagnimitra of Kalidasa. He belonged to the Baimbika family, while he was listed as a Shunga by the Puranas.

Concept note-2: -The plot. The Mālavikāgnimitra is a drama in five Acts based on a love-intrigue set on foot by King Agnimitra of Vidisa who is the Hero of the play, Mālavikā, a princess in the service of Dharini, the chief Queen, being the Heroine.

Concept note-4: -Malavikagnimitram-Characters Agnimitra was Dakshina Nayaka, who had many wives but still had feelings for Malavika, the lovely dancer. Nonetheless, he never betrays his primary wife Dharni or his ever-suspicious wife Iravati. He was a courageous King who knew how to conquer his adversaries.

Concept note-5: -Malavikagnimitra, (Sanskrit: “Malavika and Agnimitra”) five-act drama written by Kalidasa in the 5th century ce.
